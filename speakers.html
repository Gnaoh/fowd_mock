Speakers

We hand pick our speakers to bring you the best possible lineup with the most diverse range of expertise. We frequently update the site with speakers and sessions so check back for details.

Audrey An / Alexa Roman

CARBON FIVE

Audrey is a product consultant and the founder of Pantrio, a food startup based in San Francisco dedicated to minimizing food waste. Previously, she was the first mobile product manager at Prosper and Opower. She helped build Nokia's first mobile advertising platform. Prior to working in tech, Audrey studied land rights in China on a Fulbright fellowship.

Alexa is a Product Designer for Carbon Five, a collaborative design and development firm based in San Francisco. She's worked with Toyota, Macys.com, Deloitte, Downtown Santa Monica and the Sandy Hook Promise. Alexa is a design contributor to the open source project, Webcompat.com, a bug reporting tool for cross-browser compatibility. Previously, she was at Neo and Kluge helping innovation groups run experiments and creating web strategies for startups. Before working in tech, she worked in the art departments of television shows, most notably Dexter and The Office.

  • @carbonfive
  • carbonfive.com

Brian Graves

DEG

A UI designer and developer who's passionate about design and the process behind it, Brian is a strong proponent of iterative work, collaborative efforts, and user first design. He is currently the UI team lead at DEG where he does cross-channel design and development work for a range of major brands. He is the creator of Responsive Email Patterns, a key developer of MiddleMail and contributes to the Yeo+Lab project. When not immersed in design, Brian can be found around Kansas City trying out new dinner and drink spots or hanging out at home with his girlfriend and two miniature Australian Shepherds.

  • @briangraves
  • degdigital.com

  • etsy.com

Donna Lichaw

For over 15 years, Donna has helped businesses such as Atlantic Records, Citi, Bloomberg, Seamless, Apartment Therapy, and WNYC to create, launch, and innovate digital products, campaigns, and services. Emphasizing a unique mix of storytelling, design strategy, and product development, she drives teams to think big and get measurable results by starting small: story first. In addition to running her product strategy and consulting practice, she is on the adjunct faculty at Parsons The New School for Design in New York City. Donna is currently writing a book for Rosenfeld Media about using narrative architecture to design successful products and services.

  • @dlichaw

Erica Katrak / Lionel Mohri

INTUIT

Erica is a designer, researcher, developer, occasional speaker, aesthete, and perpetual learner. She's currently a creative-type-person at Intuit, where her challenge is to create the world’s best tools for running a small business. She's also one half of the team behind Krypton and Krypton Anonymous, the most private web browsing experience on the market. Prior to that, she led the design and development of a new pattern library at eBay, was part of a three-person team who led the system redesign of ebay.com, and was a design and development gun-for-hire for clients like Method Home, Pearson Education, and Stanford University. Her work has been recognized by FastCompany, The Next Web, TechCrunch, XDA, The Verge, Android Authority, Huffington Post, and many others. She spends her free time trying to become a good yogi and building boats out of scrap material.

Lionel is head of the design research and strategy team at Intuit. His team is responsible for accelerating innovation through insights and innovation concepts. Prior to his current role, Lionel played a key role in building design thinking capabilities across Intuit. He has also played a strategic role in setting the vision for various business units across the organization. Before joining Intuit in 2011, Lionel spent more than 6 years at global design consultancy IDEO where he led innovation programs ranging from designing needle-free vaccination devices to driving change in large, complex organizations. Lionel finds his energy in building creative confidence in others so that they can reach their full potential. He is also the co-creator of the Booyah Belt, a belt designed to help travelers speed through airport security. Lionel has lived on three different continents, played semi-pro Rugby in Europe, and is currently training in Krav Maga, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and MMA.

  • @intuit
  • intuit.com

Jen Simmons

THE WEB AHEAD

Jen is a designer who builds stuff, too. She’s the host and executive producer of The Web Ahead, a podcast about changing technologies and the future of the web. Creating websites since 1996, Jen's clients have included CERN, the W3C, Google, Apress, the Annenberg Foundation, and the New York Stock Exchange. In 2010, Jen designed and created the new default theme for Drupal 7, named Bartik. Besides designing for the web, Jen has 20 years experience designing for live performance and for print. Back in the day, she’d make the poster for a film festival one day, mix the sound for a live salsa band the next, and hang a photo exhibit the week after, all while designing the lighting for an upcoming play. In 2006, she created seven-channel digital projections for an opera about Nikola Tesla for the BAM Next Wave Festival. She directed several short films that toured film festivals around the globe, including screening with RESFEST and on MTV Television. She taught video-making to high school kids in San Antonio and film production to college students in Philadelphia. Jen has a MFA in Film and Media Arts from Temple University. She lives in New York City.

  • @jensimmons
  • thewebahead.net
